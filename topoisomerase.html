Meet the Enzymes

Topoisomerase

Topoisomerases untangle and reduce the tension of DNA strands in the cell

Each of your cells contains about 2 meters of DNA, all folded into the tiny space inside the nucleus, which is a million times smaller. These long, thin strands can get tangled very easily in the busy environment of the nucleus. DNA is a double helix, which must be unwound to access the genetic information. To help with these problems, your cells build several different topoisomerase enzymes that untangle and relax DNA strands.
