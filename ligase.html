Ligase

DNA ligase reconnects broken DNA strands

Gaps--breaks in one strand of the DNA--are created when the DNA is replicated. Polymerases only work in one direction when they copy a DNA strand, so one of the two strands is copied in a series of small pieces that need to be linked up into a continuous strand. DNA ligase reconnects DNA strands when they are broken.
