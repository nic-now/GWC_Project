Helicase

DNA helicase pries apart the two strands in a DNA double helix

In order to use the information inside the double helix of DNA, the helix must be unwound to expose the bases, allowing polymerases access to build complementary strands. The interaction between bases is quite strong and there are many, many of them, so it takes appreciable energy to separate the strands. This is the job of DNA helicases: they are enzymes that pull apart the two strands in a DNA double helix. The helicase surrounds a single-stranded portion of the DNA and inches its way along the strand, separating the double helix as it goes.
