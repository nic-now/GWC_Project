Polymerase

DNA polymerase makes an accurate copy of the cell's genome

DNA polymerase plays the central role in the processes of life. It is responsible for duplicating our genetic information. Each time a cell divides, DNA polymerase duplicates all of its DNA, and the cell passes one copy to each daughter cell. After it copies each base by matching cyosine to guanine and adenine to thymine, it proofreads the bases of the newly synthesized strand and cuts it out if the base is wrong.
